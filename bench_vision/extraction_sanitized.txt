===== ENTITY IDENTIFICATION (CRITICAL - READ CAREFULLY) =====

⚠️ THIS IS THE MOST COMMON ERROR - PAY CLOSE ATTENTION ⚠️

Invoices have TWO different companies/entities:
1. SUPPLIER/VENDOR (Emittente) = Company ISSUING/SENDING the invoice → EXTRACT THIS ONE
2. CUSTOMER/CLIENT (Cliente) = Company RECEIVING the invoice → IGNORE THIS ONE

COMMON MISTAKE: Extracting the CUSTOMER's data instead of the SUPPLIER's data.

HOW TO IDENTIFY THE SUPPLIER (the one to extract):
- Look for: "Emittente", "Da:", "Fornitore", "Venditore", "Cedente", "Mittente"
- Usually has company logo/letterhead at the TOP of the invoice
- Usually in the TOP-LEFT or HEADER section
- Is the company whose invoice number this is (they created the invoice)
- Their VAT appears near THEIR company name, not in "Destinatario" section

HOW TO IDENTIFY THE CUSTOMER (IGNORE this entity):
- Look for: "Cliente", "Destinatario", "A:", "Committente", "Cessionario", "Acquirente", "Spett.le"
- Usually in a box labeled "Dati Cliente" or "Destinatario"
- Usually in the TOP-RIGHT or below the supplier section
- Is the company being billed

  [TOP-RIGHT] Destinatario: G A M SAMPLE LTD, P.IVA: IT88888888888  ← THIS IS THE CUSTOMER (IGNORE)
Extract:
  supplierName: "ExampleCorp SRL" ✓
  supplierVat: "IT00000000000" ✓
NOT:
  supplierName: "G A M SAMPLE LTD" ✗ (this is the customer!)
  supplierVat: "IT88888888888" ✗ (this is the customer's VAT!)

VALIDATION RULE: The supplierName and supplierVat MUST belong to the SAME entity.

===== NUMBER FORMAT CONVERSION =====
Italian invoices use "." (dot) for thousands and "," (comma) for decimals (e.g., "9.999,99")
You MUST convert to international format:
1. Remove ALL thousand separators (dots, spaces, commas)
2. Replace decimal comma with decimal dot
3. Ensure exactly 2 decimal places

Examples:
- "9.999,99" -> "9999.99"
- "3.843" -> "9999.99" (assume 2 decimals)
- "1.234.567,89" -> "1234567.89"
- "50" -> "50.00"

Common OCR errors to avoid:
- Don't confuse "3" with "8"
- Don't confuse "5" with "S"
- Don't confuse "1" with "l" or "I"
- Don't confuse "0" with "O"
